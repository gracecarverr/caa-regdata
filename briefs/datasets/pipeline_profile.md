## Part A — row-level source, real (non-placeholder) violations, 2005–2025 (57,838 rows)

**Scale**: of the raw file's 66,655 rows, 7,193 are EPA-generated placeholders (PL1, no `VIOL_START_DATE`),
leaving 59,462 real violations; 57,838 of those fall in the 2005–2025 window, across **18,529 facilities**.

### A.1 HPV / FRV split

| tier | n | % |
|---|--:|--:|
| FRV | 40,708 | 70.4% |
| HPV | 17,130 | 29.6% |

FRV outnumbers HPV more than 2:1 — this is the population `hpv_spells` (dataset 2) excludes entirely by
design (H1).

### A.2 Linkage rates — how often a violation traces to a known evaluation or enforcement action

| measure | n | % of real violations |
|---|--:|--:|
| Linked to a known evaluation | 22,707 | 39.3% |
| Linked to an enforcement action | 40,486 | 70.0% |
| — of which, discovered via self-disclosure | 3,243 | 14.3% of linked evals |

**Most violations (60.7%) have no recorded originating evaluation** — either genuinely found some other way
(self-disclosure, a citizen complaint, a data-completeness gap in the source, etc.), or the eval-linkage
field itself is a recent addition (see A.3 — this is the more likely story). Enforcement linkage is much
higher (70.0%): most violations in this source do eventually connect to a recorded enforcement action.

### A.3 ⚠ Evaluation linkage is essentially absent before 2015 — a real pattern, not a bug

Checked directly against the row-level source (not an artifact of the facility-year collapse): `EVAL_FLAG`
is **`Y` on exactly 0 of 449 real violations dated 2005–2014**, then 6 of 103 in 2015, jumping to 573 of
3,317 in 2016 and climbing through the thousands every year after. Enforcement-action linkage (`EA_FLAG`)
shows no comparable cliff. This lines up with this repo's own Wayback coverage (2015–2025 only, dataset 1)
and looks like the same institutional fact: ECHO's evaluation↔violation linkage tracking only becomes
reliable from roughly 2015–2016 on. **Any analysis using `N_VIOL_WITH_EVAL` (or the eval-linkage share)
before 2015 will read as a true zero when it is actually "not tracked yet"** — this is a real data-coverage
limit, not a `pipeline_observed`-style unknown, since the rows are present and `EVAL_FLAG` is well-formed
`Y`/`N` throughout; it's the underlying source data that appears to a genuinely change character in 2015.

### A.6 Eval→violation and violation→enforcement lag (days)

| | n | min | p25 | median | p75 | p90 | max | mean |
|---|--:|--:|--:|--:|--:|--:|--:|--:|
| Eval → violation | 21,244 | 0 | 0 | 14 | 90 | 125 | 7,387 | 59.3 |
| Violation → enforcement | 36,586 | 0 | 0 | 62 | 210 | 471.5 | 4,054 | 171.1 |

Both distributions are heavily right-skewed (median far below mean) with a long tail — the eval→violation
lag is usually fast (median 2 weeks: an inspection that finds a violation typically reports it quickly), the
violation→enforcement lag is much slower (median ~2 months, p90 nearly 16 months) — consistent with formal
enforcement being a longer administrative process than the initial finding. The maxima (7,387 days ≈ 20
years; 4,054 days ≈ 11 years) are plausible for very old, slow-moving cases but not screened here — same
"carry the raw dates as parsed" discipline as `hpv_spells` (H4).

### A.7 `EA_PENALTY_AMT` among real violations

n = 57,838 real violations; **13,339 (23.1%) carry a positive linked penalty**, min \$1, median \$6,600, p90
\$54,775, max \$47.8M, **total \$1,048,237,359**.

**⚠ Do not add this to ds 3's penalty total.** `penalties.csv.gz`'s in-window (2005–2025) sum is
**\$4,603,442,510** — pipeline's \$1.05B is about 23% of that figure, consistent with pipeline being a
*partial* view (its own 20,251-facility source coverage, plus the pre-2015 linkage gap in A.3) rather than a
second, independent ledger of enforcement dollars. Per PL4, reconciling the two (matching `EA_ACTIVITY_ID`/
`EA_FEA_ACTIVITY_ID` against ds 3's `ENF_IDENTIFIER`) is left undone.

## Part B — `pipeline.csv.gz` (dataset 6, facility × year, 5,863,431 rows)

**Zero/NA breakdown** (mirrors dataset 0's discipline, PL3): 31,796 observed facility-years (0.54% of the
full 279,211 × 21 rectangle), 5,831,635 unobserved (`NA` throughout) — **18,529 facilities are ever
pipeline-observed**. This is a much lower observation rate than `regulatory` (12.8%) or `hpv_active`
(12.9% non-`NA`) — the pipeline source covers a narrower slice of the full ICIS universe (20,251 of 279,211
facilities even have one pipeline row at all).

### B.2 By-year pattern (full table: `output/pipeline_profile/by_year_summary.csv`)

| year | n obs. fac-yrs | % HPV | % linked to eval | % linked to EA |
|---|--:|--:|--:|--:|
| 2005 | 23 | 66.7% | 0.0% | 81.8% |
| 2010 | 24 | 90.6% | 0.0% | 84.4% |
| 2015 | 52 | 92.2% | 5.8% | 64.1% |
| 2016 | 1,872 | 32.2% | 17.3% | 79.0% |
| 2020 | 2,824 | 27.8% | 43.6% | 68.0% |
| 2025 | 3,053 | 17.6% | 42.3% | 57.5% |

Two things jump out, both real (A.3, confirmed at the row level, not an aggregation artifact):

- **The eval-linkage cliff** — 0.0% before 2015, then a steady climb to ~40–45% by 2020 onward. Consistent
  with a source-tracking change, not a behavior change (A.3).
- **HPV's share of tracked violations falls sharply** as coverage matures — 90–100% in the sparse
  pre-2015 years, down to ~18–32% from 2016 on. Read this as the reverse of A.3's story: in the thin early
  years, only the most severe (HPV) violations were making it into this extract at all; once FRV tracking
  caught up post-2015/2016, the far more numerous FRV tier (A.1) came to dominate the observed mix. **Not
  evidence that violation severity actually shifted** — a coverage-composition effect, same caution as
  `regulatory_dataset_profile.md` §1's coverage-drift note.

## Notable things to sanity-check before relying on this dataset for a headline number

- **The eval-linkage cliff (A.3/B.2) is the central caveat of this dataset** — `N_VIOL_WITH_EVAL` (and any
  rate built from it) is not meaningfully comparable before vs. after ~2015; treat pre-2015 eval-linkage as
  "not yet tracked," not "zero linked evaluations."
- **The HPV/FRV mix shift (B.2) is a coverage artifact, not a severity trend** — don't read the declining HPV
  share as violations becoming less severe over time.
- **`EA_PENALTY_AMT_SUM` (A.7) is NOT additional money on top of `penalties.csv.gz`** — it is very likely the
  same underlying dollars, seen through a narrower, partially-overlapping lens (PL4). Never sum both.
- **Only 20,251 of 279,211 facilities (7.3%) ever appear in the raw pipeline source at all** — this dataset
  is far sparser than `regulatory`/`hpv_active`; don't expect broad facility coverage from it.
