## Coverage funnel

| gate | n facilities | % of 279,211 |

| All facilities | 279,211 | 100% |

"Checkable" is smaller than "has coordinate" because a facility needs both a coordinate *and* an
ICIS-claimed county to compare against (`STATE`/`COUNTY_NAME`). "Gross error" is a subset of "checkable," not
an additional filter on the funnel.

## Coordinate-vs-ICIS-county agreement

Of the 224,921 checkable facilities, **218,892 (97.3%) land exactly in the ICIS-claimed county** (distance =
0). Of the 6,029 with a nonzero distance: concentrated near 0, tapering out to the 5km gross-error threshold
— median 0km (driven by the 97.3% exact matches), p90 0km, **p99 ≈ 7.8km**. Only 1.3% of checkable facilities
exceed 5km, i.e. a genuine geocoding/county-assignment mismatch.

## Geography

A map of the 234,640 facilities (99.5% of all facilities with a coordinate) falling within a contiguous-US
bounding box shows the expected population/industrial-density pattern — dense clusters around major metro
areas and industrial corridors (Gulf Coast, Rust Belt, Northeast corridor), sparser in the Mountain West.
234,640 of 235,919 facilities with any coordinate fall in this bounding box; the remaining ~1,279 are
AK/HI/PR/territories, excluded from the map plot only, not from the underlying data.

**Coordinate coverage varies substantially by state** — not uniform:

| state | facilities | % has coordinate | % gross error (of checkable) |
|---|--:|--:|--:|
| CO | 32,397 | 71.2% | 0.5% |
| IL | 28,445 | 93.2% | 0.1% |
| OK | 18,359 | 91.8% | 0.2% |
| **LA** | 13,834 | **29.1%** | 0.8% |
| **NY** | 12,623 | 97.2% | **7.3%** |
| PA | 11,406 | 90.3% | 0.4% |
| MD | 11,119 | 98.9% | 0.2% |
| **NM** | 8,783 | **57.3%** | 1.0% |

Two things stand out enough to flag: **Louisiana's coordinate coverage is only 29.1%**, far below every other
top-10 state (all others are 57%+) — worth checking whether this is an FRS-linkage gap specific to LA
facilities before relying on LA geography in this dataset. **New York's gross-error rate (7.3%) is roughly an
order of magnitude higher than its peers** (most top-10 states are under 1%) despite near-complete coordinate
coverage (97.2%) — a coordinate-quality issue distinct from the coverage gap seen in LA/NM/CO. Full state
table: `output/coordinates_profile/coverage_by_state.csv`.

## Summary

- Coordinate coverage (84.5%) and county-match accuracy (97.3% exact, 1.3% gross error of checkable) are both
  solid in the aggregate, but **coverage is state-dependent, not a uniform gap** — Louisiana and New Mexico
  lag well behind peers of similar facility count.
- New York's elevated gross-error rate is worth a closer look before treating NY coordinates as uniformly
  reliable, even though its raw coverage rate looks fine.
- These are the same coordinate-quality diagnostics used by the facility spine (`coord_county_flag.R`,
  shared helper) — this dataset just carries them over the full 279,211-facility universe rather than the
  spine's ever-active subset.
